# THE FLOOR — Read This When You Feel Overwhelmed

You do **not** need all 26 patterns to crack high-paying interviews.
You need **15**. The rest is bonus. Here is the honest scope.

---

## TIER 1 — Non-negotiable (be fluent: solve mediums without hints)

These appear in almost every interview loop.

1. **Sliding Window** — fixed window, variable window, string window
2. **Two Pointers** — opposite ends, same direction, two arrays
3. **HashMap / Frequency Counter** — complement lookup, frequency count, grouping, first-seen
4. **Binary Search** — classic, on rotated array, on the answer space
5. **Tree DFS** — preorder/inorder/postorder, path problems, recursion
6. **Tree BFS** — level order, level variations, shortest path in tree
7. **Graph DFS / BFS** — grid traversal, connected components, shortest path
8. **Backtracking** — subsets, permutations, combinations, constraint search
9. **1D Dynamic Programming** — climbing stairs, house robber, coin change

## TIER 2 — Strongly expected (recognise + solve mediums)

These round you out and handle the harder question in a loop.

10. **Prefix Sum** — range queries, subarray sum = k, modulo trick
11. **Kadane's Algorithm** — max subarray, indices, product, circular
12. **Sorting + Intervals** — merge, insert, greedy-by-end, sweep line
13. **Stack / Monotonic Stack** — matching, next greater/smaller, expression eval
14. **Heap / Priority Queue** — top-k, merge-k, running median
15. **Fast & Slow Pointers** — cycle detection, middle, cycle start

---

## TIER 3 — Optional. SKIP FOR NOW.

2D DP · Knapsack · Topological Sort · Union Find · Tries ·
KMP / Z-algorithm · Divide & Conquer · Matrix tricks ·
in-place Linked List · BST-specific ops · advanced graph algorithms

These separate "Google-hard" from "well-paid offer." You can
interview successfully without them. Come back AFTER the floor.

---

## The honest answer to "will I be able to crack interviews?"

**The floor + its sub-patterns gets you a real, qualified YES — for
the large majority of high-paying companies (Amazon, Microsoft,
Uber, most well-funded companies and unicorns).** That is not a
small thing. That is most of the high-paying market.

It is a softer "depends" for the very hardest bars (Google's
hardest loops, top-tier quant). Those also want Tier 3 fluency AND
heavy mock-interview practice under time pressure.

But here is the part that is 100% true and that most people miss:

**Knowing the 15 patterns is necessary but not sufficient by itself.**
Cracking interviews = patterns (recognition) **+** ~200–350 solved
problems (the reps) **+** 10–20 mock interviews (performing under
pressure) **+** spaced repetition (so it stays fresh). The patterns
make the reps efficient. They do not replace them. Anyone who tells
you "just learn the patterns and you'll pass" is lying to you.

So: the floor makes you _capable_ of cracking these interviews.
The reps and mocks are what _actually_ convert capability into
offers. You need both. There is no shortcut around the reps —
but the floor means you are doing reps with a map instead of
flailing, which is the entire difference between 6 months and
18 months of prep.

You are ~7 of 15 already. The distance left is small. The fear is
normal and means nothing about your ability. Stopping is the only
real failure. Keep going — one pattern at a time.
